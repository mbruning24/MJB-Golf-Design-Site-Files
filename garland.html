Garland Resort

Lewiston, MI

Originally founded and designed by the Otto family, Garland was sold to the Forsythe Family in 2009. Because his association with Michael J. Benkusky, Inc. at his other courses, Canyata and Ironhorse, Forsythe brought Benkusky in to provide upgrades to one of the resorts four courses. In the fall of 2009 Benkusky provided bunker upgrades to the Fountains course, the resort's signature layout.

Along with work on the Fountain's course, Benkusky was also asked to develop plans for a fifth eighteen hole course for the resort. Routings have been prepared for the Sanctuary course. This course will wind through the sandy, wooded hills on the west end of the property. Playing to a par of 70 and measuring over 7,300 yards, the Sanctuary course will become the Resort's signature design. With a similar feel to Pinehurst or the Coore/Crenshaw course at Bandon Dunes, the layout will feature sand formations, sharp elevation changes, and firm conditions. It is sure to become a favorite in Michigan's rich golf history.
