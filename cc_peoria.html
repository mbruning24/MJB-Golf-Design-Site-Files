The Country Club of Peoria

Peoria Heights, IL

Over the last 115 years, the Club has seen its share of improvements: a new pro shop, clubhouse modifications, and golf course renovation projects. In 2006, the Club set its sites on continued improvements to the golf course with the design of a new irrigation system and improvements to the courses strategy, playability, and maintenance practices.

Michael J. Benkusky, Inc. has been working with the Club since that time by preparing a golf course Enhancement Plan. Benkusky's task on this Plan was to review the course and most notably look at how the land has become overgrown due to tree planting. WIth close to 3,500 trees on nearly 100 acres, the course condition had suffered due to shade and air circulation problems. The Club's list also included bunker additions and a tee reconstruction project.

The first phase of the project, which included fairway grading, tee modifications, and bunker additions, was completed in the fall of 2007. The second phase was completed in the fall of 2008 and included closing the golf course to regrass all fairways and greens, reconstruct and level all tees, and bunker additions. The golf course re-opened in Spring 2009. This work has helped the Club return to its past glory and restore itself as the jewel of Peoria golf.
